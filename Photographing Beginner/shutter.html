Shutter

Introduction

Shutter is one of the three basic elements for photographing. The other two elements are ISO and Aperture. Shutter is a measurement of how fast a camera close the lens' gate to finish photographing taking. General shutter range is from 30 seconds (the slowest) to 1/4000 seconds (the fastest) or even more. If more than 30 seconds is needed, an external shutter cord is needed.

Range: 30, 20, 15, 10, 8, 6, ...... 1/100, 1/150, 1/200, ...... 1/1000, 1/1250, 1/2500, 1/4000 ......

Caution

The fastest shutter is not always the best and the slowest shutter is not always the worst. It indeed depends on situations. The speed of shutter also affects the amount of light received. The fastest the shutter is, the less amount of light receives. If we use a fast shutter in a very dark environment, the picture will be so dark.

Security Shutter

The biggest enemy against Shutter is hand tremor. Every photographer hate taking a very good picture but with a hand-tremor result. What a waste, right? There is term that every photographer can follow and it highly avoids hand tremor for most of the time. The term is Security Shutter. Security Shutter range is from 1/60 (someone even say for 1/100) to 1/200. This range can take a lot of general and normal picture. Certainly, this range does not 100% ensure you to totally avoid hand tremor, but a good guideline to follow. Shutter which goes larger than the security may cause users to be aware of because it will easily to cause had-tremor pictures.

Also, using a tripod is a very good choice for preventing hand tremor.

Conclusion

Therefore, shutter is a measure of speed for a camera to take a picture. The larger the number is for the shutter, the slower the shutter is and the more light the camera receives.

REMEMBER: Determine your shutter based on situations and experiences. Do not just use fast shutter to take all pictures.
