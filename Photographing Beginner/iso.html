ISO

Introduction

ISO is one of the basic elements for photographing. The other two are Aperture and Shutter. ISO is the sensitivity to detect light. The higher number the ISO is, the more sensitive the camera will detect. The usual ISO range for a camera is the following: 100, 200, 400, 800, 1600, 3200 and 6400 and all up to 25,600. Some cameras may have a higher or lower range of ISO.

Caution

Please be careful! It is not that high ISO is always better than low ISO. Both have their advantages and disadvantages. Higher ISO will produce lower quality of a picture.

Comparison

Compare with ISO 400 (figure 1.1) and ISO 6400 (figure 1.2), we can see that the figure 1.1 has a high and very clear quality for every spot in the picture. On the other hand, there are some obvious dot on figure 1.2. It is the difference about the quality of a picture with using an appropriate ISO.

When to use low and high ISO

The appropriate situations to use low ISO

  • The appropriate situations to use low ISO are every situation with very sufficient light. As there is sufficient light, we do not need a high sensitivity of ISO. Lower ISO is sufficient for a picture in order to keep the best quality of a picture.

The appropriate situations to use high ISO

  • The appropriate situations to use high ISO is that there is not enough light in the environment. Never, never use high ISO except there is not enough light. High ISO is the last solution to provide sufficient light for a picture.

Conclusion

Therefore, ISO is a scale for the needing sensitivity for a picture. The higher the ISO is, the greater the sensitivity is but the lower quality of a picture will become.

REMEMBER: Keep ISO low as much as possible!
